Dando Family
Main menu
The
Dando Family
lived at
Tyneham
from
1926
to
c. 1929
Joseph Alfred ‘Joe’ Dando (1912-2002)
Joe was born at
Radstock
, Somerset on 29 March 1912 and baptised there on 26 April 1912. He was the eldest son of miner
Oliver James Dando (1888-1941)
and his wife
Ada Maud Dando nee Butler (1888-1948)
.
Joe had four brothers, one who died shortly after birth, and a sister. However, Joe left home aged 14 before his three youngest brothers and sister were born.
Joe worked as a Servant for the Bond Family at
Tyneham House
from 1926 until 1930. It was there he met fellow servant
Alice Edith ‘Rose’ Wellman
, daughter of Bob & Alice Wellman.
Joe then worked for
Major Charles Patrick Holroyd
at
Langton House, Langton Long near Blandford
.
Joe worked at Langton House. It was demolished in 1951.
After the Major’s death in 1932, his widow
Mrs Coral Holroyd
moved to
Fritham
in the New Forest and Joe worked for her there. Meanwhile Rose secured employment in
Lyndhurst
to be nearer Joe and she was then invited by Mrs Holroyd to be her housemaid at Fritham to be with Joe.
Joe and Rose married in 1934.
Joe and Rose had
twelve children
– seven boys and five girls. Sadly their first-born child
Anthony G Dando
(1934-1944) died at the age of just nine.
Joe and Rose worked for a short while for
Cicely Yeatman
(William Henry Bond’s eldest daughter)
at
Stock Gaylard
.
By September 1939 Joe, Rose and son Anthony were at
Steeple Leaze Farm
. Joe, a farm worker, was also cook for the evacuation camp and Rose, performing unpaid domestic duties, was a St. John’s first aider.
In 1949, Joe was employed as a
dairyman
by Mr A Vickery of
Kimmeridge Farm
.
Rose died on 11 May 1992 aged 79 and was buried at Kimmeridge. Joe died on 5 January 2002 aged 89.
