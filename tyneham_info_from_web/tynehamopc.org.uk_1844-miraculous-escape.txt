Poole, Saturday, April 13
MIRACULOUS ESCAPE. –
Lieut. Wilmot, R.N.
, of the
Warbarrow Coast Guard Station
, received some severe injuries about a fortnight ago whilst on night duty, he having (owing to the extreme darkness of the night) walked over the highest part of
Gadcliff
, which is an elevation of 128 feet. The first portion of the cliff is about 40 feet perpendicular, the remainder being on the slope, both rugged and rocky; he was shortly after being picked up by one of his men, who fortunately heard him call for help, and by the assistance of others , was carefully cenveyed [
conveyed
] to his residence.
Hampshire Advertiser & Salisbury Guardian, Saturday 13 April 1844
