The Bungalow
Hill Cottage, which stood alongside the
Coastguard Station
, was home to generations of the ‘fishing Millers’. ‘Old Henry Miller’, his bachelor son Tom, and many of their ancestors had been deeply involved in smuggling whilst living next door to the officers charged with stamping out the trade.
