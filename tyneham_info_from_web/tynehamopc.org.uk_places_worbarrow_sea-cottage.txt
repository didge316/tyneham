The Bungalow
Sea Cottage
stood proudly close to the shoreline of
Worbarrow Bay
. In
1840
, when the
Tithe Map
was prepared, it was owned and occupied by
Samuel Miller (1763-1848)
and his wife
Elizabeth Miller nee Bradford (1763-1846)
. Samuel was the grandson of Worbarrow resident
Henry Miller (1680-1747)
. Subsequent generations of the
Miller family
lived at
Sea Cottage
until the forced
evacuation
in
December 1943
.
Sea Cottage from the shoreline c. 1910
Sea Cottage, occupied by the Joseph Millers and afterwards by the Jacks, stood close to the shore with nothing but a narrow, buttressed path between it and the shingle.
Lilian Bond
Sea Cottage c.1930
The derelict cottage is featured on Tyneham Action Group postcards
Sea Cottage stood above the rocky beach alongside the boathouse slipway, facing the South Westerly gales. For over thirty years it was home to Old Henry Miller’s son
Jack Miller
, his wife ‘Miggie’ (Alice White Rose) and her mother Martha, a lively, quick witted East Ender known to all as Granny Rose.
In 1943 Jack and ‘Miggie’, both aged 77, were evacuated to a cottage at Langton Matravers near Swanage.
Only the foundation bank now remains
