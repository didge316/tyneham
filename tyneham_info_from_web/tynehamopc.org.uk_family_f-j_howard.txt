The
Howard Family
lived at
North Egliston Farm
, Tyneham for
22 years
from
1906
until
1928
Charles William Howard (1860-1928)
Charles was born on 26 November 1860 at
Lower Bockhampton
, Dorset. His parents were Suffolk-born
William Howard (1821-1906)
, a dairyman, and his second wife
Maria Howard nee Suttle (1825-1905)
.
Charles married
Emily Alice Tewksbury (1861-1936)
, known as
Alice
, at Portsea, Hampshire in 1887. Alice was born on 9 September 1861 at Portsea.
Photo courtesy of Scott Green
Charles and Alice had seven children, one of whom died shortly after birth:
William Albert Howard (1887-1954)
–
Charles Gordon ‘Gordon’ Howard (1887-1967)
–
Henry Wilton Howard (1889-1889)
– died in infancy
Francis Lee Suttle ‘Frank’ Howard (1890-1958)
–
Alice Daisy Maria ‘Daisy’ Howard (1892-1976)
–
George Frederick Howard (1893-1956)
–
Eva May Howard (1895-1972)
– Eva was born on 13 September 1895 at Ringstead, Dorset – she married
Percy Ernest Edgar Chilcott (1889-1985)
at Tyneham on 28 April 1918
Charles was the brother-in-law of former farmer
Frederick Thomas Way (1864-1943)
and had taken over
North Egliston Farm
by 1906.
In 1917, Charles was elected as Overseer of the Poor.
Charles died at Tyneham on 28 November 1928. He was buried in Stratton churchyard on 1 December 1928. Alice later lived at Godlingston Farm and died on 14 April 1936 at the Cottage Hospital, Swanage. Alice was buried at Tyneham (in a very fancy grave).
Alice Daisy Maria ‘Daisy’ Howard (1892-1976)
Photo courtesy of Scott Green
Daisy lived at Blashenwell Farm, Kingston, Dorset. She married
Harry Stephens
at St Mark’s Church, Portsmouth on 23 August 1910
1
. Harry was the son of the late
John Stevens (1848-1915)
and
Mary Stevens nee Savage (1848-1898)
, of Eastington Farm, Swanage. By 1911
Harry Stephens
was farming Blashenwell. He had then been married for less than a year to wife
Daisy
.
Photo courtesy of Scott Green
Harry Stevens
served as a Private with the Dorsetshire Regiment, 2nd Battalion (service number 27367). Sadly Harry died aged 34 in captivity as a prisoner-of-war on 16 July 1917 at Basra Hospital after suffering sunstroke. He is commemorated at Baghdad (North Gate) War Cemetery, Iraq (grave/memorial reference XV. C. 11.) and also on the
Swanage War Memorial
and in the churches at Kingston and Worth Matravers churches.
Harry and Daisy had a son
Laurie Howard Stevens (1912-2005)
and twins
May Howard Stevens
and
Edward Howard Stevens
both of whom sadly died shortly after birth in 1914.
Photo courtesy of Lisa Leahy
Photo courtesy of Lisa Leahy

Laurie Howard Stevens (1912-2005)
Laurie Howard Stevens, known mostly as Howard, was born on 28 March 1912.
Howard married
Nancy Marianne Farthing (1922–2006)
at Church Knowle, Dorset on 15 July 1943. Nancy was born on 1 November 1922 in the Ealing area. Her father was
Revd. Robert Farthing
of St Peters, Church Knowle.
Footage of the wedding recorded on Cine film can be found here http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Lacq4BtHaFs
Laurie died Nancy died on 2 May 2006. She was buried at Church Knowle.
