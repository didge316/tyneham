TITLE: RAF Brandy Bay - Tyneham
============================================================
RAF Brandy Bay
Extract from
Frontline Dorset, A County at War 1939-45
, by
George Forty
, 1994:
There were a significant number of RAF-manned radar stations in Dorset
. . .
A
WAAF
stationed at one of these units was
Gabrielle Holyoake
, who was on the Purbeck coast, at Tyneham, between Lulworth Cove and St Alban’s Head. The site was called
RAF Brandy Bay
, and she was manning a
Type 7000 GEE navigational radar
, which was used to help guide our Bomber crews all over Europe.
. . .
Gabrielle was prevented by lack travel facilities and the Official Secrets Act from knowing the exact whereabouts of the other stations in the Southern chain, however, she did know the master was at Bulbarrow Hill, near Blandford, while there were slaves at Truleigh Hill on the Downs behind Shoreham-by-Sea and at West Prawle near Salcombe in Devon, with the monitor being at RAF Brandy Bay. The first famous success for GEE was the 1,000 bomber raid on Cologne on 31 May 1942. She goes on:
. . .
Brandy Bay was one of the happiest of RAF stations. There were only about 40 of us, the girls lived in
Tyneham House
, then a beautiful Elizabethan manor, whilst the men lived in the
rectory
in Tyneham village.
. . .
We had little spit and polish, no drill or parades. Off duty we made our own amusements, being free to roam on
Gad Cliff
,
Tyneham Gap
and over to Kimmeridge Bay. We ignored the invasion defences to swim in
Worbarrow Bay
. Until we acquired bicycles the only way to Corfe Castle was to walk the five miles. We hitchhiked to Swanage and Wareham and once a month we were able to go on the laundry run to Bournemouth (my home town). Over Whiteways Hill was the Lulworth army camp where we went to dances. We occasionally organised a dance at
Tyneham House
, but we only had a gramaphone and a radio!
I have happy memories of those years. I cannot remember it ever raining or being cold. Friendships were made which have survived to this day.
Gabrielle Holyoake
