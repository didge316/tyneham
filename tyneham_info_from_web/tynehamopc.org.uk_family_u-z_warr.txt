The Warr Family
Main menu
The
Warr Family
lived at
Tyneham
in
1888
and at
Worbarrow
from
1911
until
1919
The ‘Warr’ family tiles in Tyneham Church
Edwin Charles Warr (c.1862-1937)
Edwin was born at
Sturm
inster Newton
, Dorset circa 1862 to
Elizabeth Warr nee Eustance
. Her husband
George Warr (1821-1858)
had died shortly before the birth of their daughter
Amelia
in 1858. There does not appear to be any civil registration or church baptism record for Edwin.
At the time of the 1871 census, Edwin aged 9 was living at
Belchalwell
, Dorset with his mother and step-father.
Edwin, who could not write, gave his father’s name as Charles Warr when he married (see below). Edwin’s mother Elizabeth married
James Elsworth
and they had a son William.
Edwin married
Sarah Elmes (1865-1937)
at
Tyneham Church
on 21 February 1887. Edwin’s occupation was labourer and his residence was given as Steeple. He signed with his mark. Sarah’s residence was given as Tyneham. The witnesses were
George Elmes (1861-1894)
, Sarah’s eldest brother, and
Emma Murray (1855-?)
.
Marriage at Tyneham of Edwin to Sarah
From 29 April 1889, Edwin was
licensee
of the
Red Lion
,
Charleton Horethorne
, Somerset. It was owned by
Jennings, Styring & Co
of the Marnhull Brewery, Sturminster Newton and is understood to have closed in 1891.
On 3 April 1890, Edwin and Sarah departed Liverpool with their two children, Charles and Elizabeth, on board the
SS Gulf of Corcovado
bound for
Valparaíso
, Chile
. Sarah would have been expecting daughter Annie at the time.
By 1902 the family had returned from Chile and were living at
Slepe
, roughly halfway between Arne and Stoborough.  Edwin was most likely employed by
Walter Case Smith
at
Slepe Farm
.
By 2 April 1911 Edwin and Sarah and the six younger children were living at
Worbarrow
near Tyneham.

Edwin died in 1937 aged 75 and was buried at
Stratton
, Dorset on 18 January 1937. Sarah died later that same year aged 73 and was buried on 6 December 1937.
Children of Edwin & Sarah
Edwin and Sarah had a total of
15 children
, 8 of whom had died by 1911. Known children are:
Charles Warr (1888-?)
–
Charles was baptised at Tyneham on 22 April 1888 – he departed for Chile with his parents in April 1890 aged two
–
he may have died there
Bessie Elizabeth Martha Warr (1889-?)
–
Bessie was born on 26 May 1889 at the Red Lion,
Charlton Horethorne
, Somerset – Bessie was baptised at Charlton Horethorne on 7 July 1889
– s
he departed for Chile with her parents in April 1890 aged just 10 months
–
she may have died there
Annoucement of Bessie’s birth in 1889
Annie Louisa Warr (1890-1966)
–
Annie was born in Chile – while living back at Warbarrow she had a son
Edwin Charles Warr junior
born in 1913 – Annie married labourer
William Henry ‘Henry’ Parker
at Tyneham on 12 October 1915 – Henry and Annie had eight children – Henry & Annie celebrated their Golden Wedding in 1965 – Henry died in 1971 aged 78 and Annie died in 1976 aged 75
William George Warr (1892-1966)
–
William  was born in
Quilpué
, Chile – William took part in an air rifle shooting match for Tyneham against Povington in March 1914 scoring 19 points and helping them to victory – William served as a private (200518) with the
4th Dorsets
from 1915 – in 1922 he married
Muriel Gwendoline ‘Gwen’ Watts (1904-1995)
and they had eight children one of whom died shortly after birth aged 3 weeks – in 1939 the family were living at Lower Woolcombe Farm,
Melbury Bubb
– William and Gwen (also known as Granny Grimble) later lived at School Lane,
Chetnole
– William died in 1966 aged 73 and was buried at Chetnole on 12 January 1966 – Gwen died in 1995 aged 90
Henry Santiago Warr (1894-?)
–
Henry was born in Chile
and may have died there
Sarah Warr (1896-1899)
–
Sarah was born in Chile – she died there on 30 March 1899 aged 3
Albert Frank Warr (1899-1972?)
–
‘Frank’ also served as a private (201424) with the
4th Dorsets
– he may  have married a girl from Northern Ireland and settled there
Mabel Warr (1899-1900)
–
Mabel was born on 24 June 1899 at Valparaíso, Chile – sadly she died on 5 January 1900 aged just six months of diahorrea
Robert Thomas Warr (1900-?)
–
Robert was born in Chile
and may have died there
Alice Edith Ella Warr (1902-1936)
–
‘Edith’ was born at Slepe – she married
Alfred William Edward Watts (1896-1972)
in 1921 – Alfred was the sister of Muriel, William’s wife – Edith and Alfred had a daughter
Margaret
– Edith died in February 1936 aged 34 and was buried at Stratton – Alfred died in 1976 aged 72
Julia Isabel Rose Warr (1903-1997)
– Julia was born at Slepe on 6 June 1903 – she did not marry – in September 1939 she was in domestic service at Southend-on-Sea, Essex apparently also doing A.R.P. work – Julia died in 1997 aged 94
Two of the Warr sisters visiting Tyneham
Margaret Florence Warr (1905-1985)
–
Margaret was born on 12 May 1905 and known as both ‘Maggie’ and ‘May’ – she married
George Stanley Bown (1905-1978)
, known as Stanley, at Frampton in 1928 and they had three children – in 1939 the family were living at Ridgeway near Weymouth – Stanley died in 1978 aged 72 and Margaret died in 1985 aged 80
Daisy Warr (1907-1992)
–
Daisy was born at Wytch on 5 January 1907 – she was baptised at Tyneham on 7 February 1909 – Daisy had a son Edwin (known as ‘Eddie’) born in 1934 – Daisy married
Granville Preston (1898-1975)
in 1939 – Granville was introduced to Daisy by older sister Annie – Granville and Daisy had four children – sadly Alfred (known as ‘Joseph’) pictured on the left below died aged 4 – Granville died in 1975 aged 77 and Daisy died in 1992 aged 85
