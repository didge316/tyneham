Two branches of the
Cleall Family
lived at
Povington
CLEALL – BRANCH 1
John Cleall
, aged 29, married
Hannah Burden
, aged 20, on 19 April 1881 at Chaldon Herring, Dorset. John and Hannah had at least nine children, three of whom later lived at Povington:
William John Cleall (1882-1957)
–
married
Alice Child (1887-1965)
and lived in Sussex
Francis Henry Cleall (1884-1943)
–
Florence Lucy Mary ‘Lucy’ Cleall (1886-1963)
–
married William James Turner (1883-1966) in 1907
Thomas George Cleall (1888-1890)
–
died in infancy aged 22 months
Charles Job Cleall (1890-1917)
–
Daisy Annie Mabel Cleall (1892-1893)
–
died in infancy aged 12 months
Walter James Cleall (1894-1984)
–
Elizabeth Sarah Annie Cleall (1896-1981)
– married Frederick Simpson (1888-?)
Hannah died in 1900 aged 38  when her youngest daughter was just 4. John died aged 54 with his death registered in the first quarter of 1906.
Francis Henry ‘Frank’ Cleall (1884-1943)
Francis Henry ‘Frank’ Cleall
was baptised on 6 July 1884 at Chaldon Herring. In 1911 Frank, aged 26, and his two brothers
Charles
, aged 21 and
Walter
, aged 16, were boarding at
West Whiteway
.
Charles Job Cleall was killed in
WW1
and is commemorated on the memorial plaque in
Tyneham Church
.
Frank married
Frances Amelia Charles
of Steeple parish in 1912. Frank died after notice of eviction was served but before the evacuation took place.
Private CHARLES JOB CLEALL
113367, 257th Coy., Machine Gun Corps (Infantry)
who died age 27
on 02 November 1917
Son of John and Hannah Cleall.
Walter James Cleall, aged 25, married Emily Bradford, aged 28 on 12 August 1919 at Tyneham.
CLEALL – BRANCH 2
George Cleall (1883-1925)
George, born at West Lulworth, was the nephew of John Cleall (see Branch 1 above). George’s father was David Cleall (1834-1897) who married Elizabeth Mary Cheeseman (1845-1935) at Warmwell on 10 April 1867.
George married Bessie Baggs (1881-1953) at Winfrith Newburgh on 5 December 1917.
The tragic circumstances under which
George Cleall (43)
, a cowman employed at
Povington Farm
, East Lulworth, met his death whilst returning from Hethfelton Farm with a load of straw on a haorsed waggon, formed the subject of an enquiry by the Deputy Coroner for East Dorset (Mr. R. D. Maddock) at Povington Farm on Monday.
Bessie Cleall
, of
Povington
, wife of deceased, giving evidence of identification, mentioned that her husband had been working for
Mr. T. W. Wrixon
for about 16 months. Deceased was quite well when he left home about 7 a.m. on Saturday, and he had no serious illness. He never complained about his work. There were two children, one aged four years next June and the other 1 year 6 months.
