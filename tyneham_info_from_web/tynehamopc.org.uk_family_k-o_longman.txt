TITLE: Longman Family - Tyneham
============================================================
Longman Family
Main menu
The
Longman Family
lived at
Baltington Farm
Tyneham for
30 years
from around
1913
until the evacuation in December
1943
.
Albert Bernard Longman (1869-1943)
Albert was born on 17 April 1869 at
Hurn
, near Christchurch, to
Thomas Longman (1831-1905)
and his wife
Jemima Mary Longman nee Wills (1830-1916)
.
In 1871 his father was
publican
at the
Horns Inn
, Dudsbury near
West Parley
which also doubled as a general shop.
By 1881, the family were living at
Firgrove Farm
, West Parley. His father was farming 48 acres and employing one man.
By 1891, the family had progressed to
Holdenhurst Farm
. Albert, aged 21, was a
dairyman
.
By 1901, the family had moved again, this time to
Hurn Bridge Farm
. Albert was now a
Farm Bailiff
.
In 1905 Albert married
Emma Caroline Bartlett (1870–1940)
. Emma was the daughter of farmer
George Bartlett (1846-1909)
and his wife
Louisa Martha Bartlett nee Miller (1846-1907)
of
Heron Court Farm
, Hurn.
Albert and Emma had four children:
Thomas Herbrand George ‘Tommie’ Longman
(1906–1914)
– born at Hurn – died at Tyneham
Louisa Winifred Mary ‘Louie’ Longman (1908–1977)
– born at Hurn
Evelyn Mary Longman  (1910–1985)
– born at Hurn
Reginald Charles Augustine Longman  (1913–1983)
– born at Tyneham
By 1913 the family had moved to
Baltington Farm
.
In the 1920s Albert rented out one of his fields between Tyneham and Worbarrow to the Scoutmasters of
Eton College
for their week-long annual
Summer Scout Camps
.
In 1932, Albert Longman employed
Mr. S Allen
, an engine driver.
In September 1939, Albert, wife Emma, son Reginald, daughter Evelyn and housekeeper Lydia Dowsett were still living at Baltington Farm. Evelyn was a dairymaid.
Albert died on 9 December 1943 – believed to be due to the shock of seeing his cattle being sold off so cheaply at the auction.
Baltington Farm Disposal Report, Western Gazette, 17 December 1943
Albert Longman was my Grandfather and actually died on the day of his sale. He was living in Corfe with my Mum and Dad (Louie and Stanley Sheasby). He may have gone to the sale first and died later. My Mum was brought up at Baltington Farm and went to
Tyneham School
. Sadly she died before more open access was allowed in the village.
Bridget Dixon
Thomas Herbrand George ‘Tommie’ Longman  (1906–1914)
Tommie was born at Hurn in 1906. Sadly he died at Baltington Farm on 22 October 1914 aged just 8 years. It is understood he was kicked in the leg by a cow and contracted TB. Apparently villagers took a door off its hinges to stretcher him.
In Memoriam – Western Gazette 19 October 1917
Louisa Winifred Mary ‘Louie’ Longman (1908–1977)
Louie was born at
Hurn
o 9 February 1908. She married
Stanley Charles ‘Stan’ Sheasby (1904-1960)
in 1933 at St Mary’s Roman Catholic Church, East Lulworth.
Stan was the son of
Ernest Sheasby (1878-1932)
who started a horse and carriage business at
Corfe Castle
in 1906. The business expanded and motor buses were added. Two tea rooms were opened to cater for visitors. After obtaining a school contract to transport children, he purchased his first coaches. Sadly in 1932, aged 53, he was
fatally injured
after being crushed by one of his coaches at the Hants and Dorset Depot in Poole. Ernest’s family continued to operate the business and the name
South Dorset Coaches
was introduced. The family finally sold the 99 year old business in 2005.
1936 Advertisement for Sheasby’s of Corfe Castle
In September 1939, Louie was
proprietress
of the
Castle Tea Gardens
, Corfe Castle. Stan was a
bus owner
.
1
Stan and Louie had a daughter
Bridget
.
Stan died on 12 July 1960 at the Royal Victoria Hospital, Boscombe, Bournemouth aged 56. Louie died on 30 January 1977 aged 68.
Evelyn Mary Longman  (1910–1985)
Evelyn was born on 12 October 1910 at
Hurn
. She married
George Thomas ‘Cudgie’ Wright (1913-1988)
in 1940.
Reginald Charles Augustine Longman  (1913–1983)
Reginald was
born at Tyneham
on 26 November 1913.
Reginald married
Joan Mary Potter (1921-1973)
of Church Hill,
Donhead St Mary
, Wiltshire in 1943. Her father was a wheelright’s smith.
1
By June 1945 Reginald and Joan were living at
Stockford Farm
, East Stoke.
Reginald and Joan had five daughters – Rita, Patricia, Rosemarie known as Diane, Janet and Gillian.
Joan died in 1973 aged 51 and Reginald died on 15 December 1983 aged 70.
2
They are buried at East Stoke as too is daughter Diane.
I used to have a drink with Reg when he came into Wareham on Saturday nights, very often in the Black Bear Hotel. He was a very interesting man to talk to.
Martin Burgin-Thorpe
