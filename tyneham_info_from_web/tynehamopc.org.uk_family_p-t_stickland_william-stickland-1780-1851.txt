William Stickland (1780-1851)
According to the 1851 census, William was born at Steeple about 1780. The parishes of Tyneham and Steeple were linked at that time with Tyneham baptisms being duplicated in the Steeple registers. The Steeple registers do not show a William Stickland being baptised at this time, but a William Stickland, son of Joseph & Elizabeth Stickland, was baptised on 27 February 1780 at the neighbouring parish of Church Knowle.
[
A Joseph Stickland of Almer and Elizabeth Vye of West Lulworth were married at Tyneham on 5 July 1779. Elizabeth was the daughter of Thomas and Molly Vye (nee Davis) of Egliston. Joseph and Elizabeth had three later children baptised at Tyneham.
]
William married
Sarah Ann Lush (1783-1871)
at Tyneham on 11 September 1804. Sarah was born at Windmill Street in London to James and Ann Lush (nee Parsons) and was baptised at St. Marylebone on 6 April 1783. At the time of her marriage, it is believed Sarah may have been working at Smedmore House.
William and Sarah had seven children, all baptised at Tyneham:
Amey Stickland (1805-?)
– Amey was baptised  on 1 September 1805.
Louisa Stickland (1809-1868)
– Louisa was baptised on 13 August 1809. She was shown as an ‘invalid’ in the 1851 census and as a ‘cripple’ in the 1861 census. She lived with her mother.
Sophia Stickland (1811-?)
– Sophia was baptised on 22 June 1811. She married
Edward Waters
, an agricultural labourer from Woodford in Wiltshire. They had eight children all baptised at Woodford.
William Lush Stickland (1814-1881)
– William was baptised at Tyneham on 12 May 1814. Click on his name to learn much more.
Thomas Stickland (1817-1870)
– Thomas was baptised on 18 January 1817.
[He was a fisherman and, along with his elder brother, was sentenced in 1840 to six months imprisonment for smuggling.]
He married
Elizabeth Balson (1819-1911)
at Tyneham on 10 June 1846. They had six children: Fanny (1846-1913?), Francis (1848-1869), Emma Elizabeth (1851-?), Frederick (1853-1906), William (1857-?), Emily (1860-1923). By 1861, the family were living at Kimmeridge.
Jane Lush Stickland (1819-?)
– Jane was baptised on 17 October 1819.
Louis James Stickland (1822-1885)
– Louis was baptised on 26 May 1822 as James Lewis Stickland.
William died in July 1851 aged 71 and was buried at Tyneham on 24 July. At the time of the 1871 census, William’s widow Sarah was living with her grandson Louis and family at Sharnell. She died later that year aged 88 in Hertfordshire on 14 November. She was buried at Tyneham on 1 December 1871.
William was a fisherman and the 1841 census shows two households at Sharnell (Charnel) in the Parish of Kimmeridge, one with William as the head and the other with his son William, who was also a fisherman.
