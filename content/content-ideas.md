## 2. Visiting Tyneham — Practical Guide
**File name:** `visiting-tyneham.html`
**Target keywords:** visiting tyneham, tyneham visitor guide, tyneham parking, is tyneham free to visit, tyneham opening hours
**Word count:** 900–1,100
**Demand:** High — PAA questions and TripAdvisor reviews show consistent practical questions not fully answered by opening_times.html.

Key points:
- Entry free; £2 parking donation (honesty box)
- Gate opens 9am, exhibitions 10am–4pm, closes at dusk
- Red flag system — call 0800 0856628 or check GOV.UK before travelling
- Open ~137 days/year on average (weekends + school holidays + bank holidays)
- Facilities: portaloos near car park, no bins, no café — bring food and water
- Dogs allowed on leads
- No mobile signal; download offline map beforehand
- The red telephone box — most photographed feature
- Accessibility: uneven ground, no paved paths
- What to expect: 1–3 hours for village + Worbarrow Bay; half-day with circular walk

## 5. Wildlife at Tyneham
**File name:** `tyneham-wildlife.html`
**Target keywords:** tyneham wildlife, wildlife lulworth ranges, purbeck heaths nature reserve, birds tyneham dorset
**Word count:** 1,000–1,200
**Demand:** Medium–High — Purbeck Heaths NNR (2020 designation — UK's first "super" NNR) is under-covered by tourism sites.

Key points:
- Purbeck Heaths National Nature Reserve declared 2020 — 3,000+ hectares
- All 6 native UK reptiles present (adder, grass snake, smooth snake, slow worm, common lizard, sand lizard)
- Heathland birds: Dartford warbler, nightjar, woodlark, stonechat; raptors incl. peregrine, merlin, hen harrier
- Rare orchids: early spider orchid visible May–June on chalk downland above Worbarrow Bay
- The paradox: 80 years of military exclusion has protected habitat that would otherwise have been developed
- Butterflies: chalkhill blue, Adonis blue, silver-studded blue on chalk grassland
- Deer (roe and sika) frequently seen at dusk
- Best seasons to visit for wildlife

## 9. Gad Cliff
**File name:** `gad-cliff.html`
**Target keywords:** gad cliff dorset, gad cliff walk, gad cliff tyneham
**Word count:** 700–900
**Demand:** Medium — has its own Wikipedia article; appears in multiple walk itineraries from Tyneham. Currently not mentioned on the site despite being the dramatic cliff directly above the village.

Key points:
- 135m chalk and limestone cliff above Tyneham and Brandy Bay
- Part of the South West Coast Path within Lulworth Ranges
- Views west to Portland Bill, east to St Alban's Head
- Smuggling history: Brandy Bay below named for brandy kegs stored here; tubs reportedly raised to cliff top and hidden at Tyneham church
- Access from Tyneham car park via coastal path (only when ranges open)
- Iron Age earthworks on Tyneham Cap at the eastern end
